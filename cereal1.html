Welcome to the Flaked Cereal Page!

The first flaked cereal, the famous Corn Flakes, were created on accident by a Religious group known as the Seventh-day Adventists in an attempt to find a more humble food, accommodating to their beliefs. Corn Flakes as a brand was invented by Dr. John Harvey Kellog, a fellow Adventist, for use in controlling sin, specifically lust. Receiving positive reviews from patients at a Sanitarium he worked at, he decided to mass market it and formed the famous company Kellogs, starting it's legacy in the cereal business.
Usual Ingredients: Sugar, Corn, Salt and Malt Flavor.

Flaked Cereal Examples.

The first example, Corn flakes are perhaps the most recognizable flaked cereal. Invented in 1894, Corn Flakes have been the classic symbol for a standard breakfast for a century.

The second cereal in question is Frosted Flakes, invented in 1952, this classic glazed flaked cereal has become a symbol of children's breakfast for decades.

The third cereal, Raisin Bran, was an 1926 addition to the standard Corn Flake cereal, heavily debated as a "healthy alternative", Raisin Bran adds a little extra to a standard bowl of Corn Flakes.
